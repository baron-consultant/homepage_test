      The Infrastructure Construction Industry is the sector that builds essential foundations for the national economy and society, developing under the influence of legal regulations and public policy.

      Recently, the construction industry has been transforming into a new paradigm, combining elements such as digital technology, eco-friendly policies, and the revitalization of private investment.

        Building Information Modeling
        (BIM)

        BIM (Building Information Modeling)
        refers to the Digital Transformation (DX) framework that integrates all information generated throughout a facility's lifecycle based on a 3D model, allowing construction data and procedures to be interconnected in a standardized manner and enabling digital collaboration.

          Geographic Information System
          (GIS)

          GIS (Geographic Information System) is
          an information system that collects, analyzes, and visualizes geographic (spatial) data for utilization in various fields..

          It is a core technology that goes beyond simple map creation to support location-based data analysis and decision-making, making it essential across various industries such as construction, transportation, urban planning, environmental management, and disaster prevention.

            Digital Twin(DT)

            DT (Digital Twin) is
            a technology that duplicates real-world objects, systems, and processes identically in a virtual space to perform real-time monitoring, analysis, and simulation.

            It creates a digital replica identical to a physical entity such as a building, factory, city, bridge, or infrastructure to synchronize data in real-time and enable optimization.
