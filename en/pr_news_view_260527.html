    Seowon University and Baron Consultants Co., Ltd. 'Hands-on' to strengthen AI and digital capabilities in the field of environmental safety
    2026.05.28
    Seowon University signed a business agreement with Baron Consultants on the 27th to strengthen its research and design capabilities in the field of environmental safety that combines AI and digital technologies This agreement was promoted with the aim of mutual development by developing core technologies in the environmental safety field necessary in the AI era, strengthening software-based research and design capabilities, and establishing a system for fostering future environmental safety professionals and industry-academia cooperation. Through the agreement, the two institutions agreed to cooperate in the operation of the curriculum reflecting the demand for industrial sites and the development of future educational models.

    In addition, it plans to accelerate the expansion of practical industry-academic cooperation, such as internships and experience programs. In particular, in order to strengthen research and design capabilities in the environment and safety sectors, Seowon University decided to jointly utilize software and related technical information developed and provided by Baron Consultants in education, research, and industry-academic cooperation programs It is expected to serve as an opportunity to strengthen its capabilities.

    Kim Choong-gon, head of the environmental engineering department at Seowon University, said, "The environment and safety sector is a key sector required by the future society, such as carbon neutrality, resource circulation, water quality and atmospheric environmental management, and now research and design capabilities using AI and digital technologies are essential." It will be an important starting point to increase students' practical competency and job competitiveness by directly connecting the field's technology to university education and research, and to systematically cultivate future talents in the environment and safety fields, he said.

    Source: Dongyang Ilbo reporter Hong Seung-tae
