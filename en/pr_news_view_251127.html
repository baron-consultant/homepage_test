    Uiwang City Corporation Signs MOU with Baron Consultants to
    Support Innovation and Growth
    2025.11.27
    (Reporter Kim Tae-hyun) Uiwang City Corporation (President Noh Sung-hwa) signed a business agreement with Baron Consultants on the 26th to support innovation and growth in the conference room of the corporation building.

    This agreement was conducted to establish the competitiveness of local industrial competitiveness through professional exchanges between civil technology and public institutions.The implementation of the agreement, Baron Consultants consultation, provides feedback from various projects such as urban development, and urban development fields such as urban development, and urban development fields.

    Construction employees are expected to achieve improve business efficiency and capabilities through domestic self-developed programs rather than existing commercialized solutions.

    It is expected that Baron Consultants will also be able to secure competitiveness in the program by strengthening the solution function through feedback advice from the public corporation.

    President Roh Sung-hwa said, "We hope that this business agreement will develop into a private cooperation model that can bring about practical synergies in mutual development based on human and material resources between the two organizations and revitalizing the local economy. We will contribute to revitalizing the local economy and developing private companies through continuous efforts of public-private cooperation."

    Meanwhile, with this agreement, the two organizations will continue to discover mid- to long-term cooperation tasks such as △operating a practical curriculum for executives and employees △ holding a technology seminar with public and private experts △ joint discovery of digital-based work innovation tasks. Through this, it aims to create a virtuous cycle of win-win model such as fostering professional manpower in the region, supporting the growth of private companies, and improving the quality of public services.

    Source: Kim Tae-hyun, reporter of Sisa News Agency
