      BIM Characteristics
      of each Industry

      Infrastructure Construction Industry is Unlike the manufacturing and building construction industries, the Infrastructure Construction Industry lacks identical forms due to variations in basic elements like terrain and alignment, and features and processes differ according to project type, making model repetition and reuse difficult.

      Therefore, information models in the infrastructure construction industry must be newly created for every project by combining or mixing construction-specific software and various general-purpose software.

        Comparison of BIM
        for Building
        & Infrastructure Construction

        Building construction features a vertical structure with high repetition of floors and spaces, leading to a design focused primarily on clash detection between trades,

        whereas the infrastructure construction industry, due to its broad and linear horizontal structure, requires a design that comprehensively considers terrain and subgrade conditions, as well as the relationships between preceding and succeeding trades

          Digital Transformation in
          the Construction Industry
          a GIS and BIM Composite System

          To achieve Digital Transformation in the Infrastructure Construction Industry, not only 3D model-based facility (construction) information (BIM) but also location-based spatial information (GIS), including geography, terrain, and subgrade conditions, must be integrated (GSIM: Geographic and Structure Information Model) and used together.
